Leading the Development of ADR25 for OBRA

The Autonomous Design Report (ADR) is a comprehensive technical document that serves as both a record of OBRA’s autonomous system development and a detailed justification of the engineering decisions made throughout the season. Unlike a standard progress report, the ADR focuses on explaining why each method, technology, or approach was selected, comparing it to alternatives, and providing insight into how it contributes to the overall performance of the system. The goal is to create a technical reference that future teams can use to understand and build upon the work done this year.

As the lead for the development of ADR25, I have been responsible for structuring, writing, and refining the document to ensure that each subsystem is thoroughly documented and its decisions are well justified. This involves coordinating with different teams, gathering input, and ensuring the report is aligned with OBRA’s broader engineering objectives. I have facilitated discussions, reviewed drafts, and worked to maintain clarity and consistency across sections, ensuring that the ADR is not only a record of progress but also a valuable resource for future development. My role requires balancing technical accuracy with clear documentation, making sure ADR25 is an effective and well-structured guide for the autonomous system’s evolution.

Comparative Study of Delaunay and RRT* as Path Planners for Autonomous Racing

My bachelor's dissertation, titled Comparative Study of Delaunay and RRT* as Path Planners for Autonomous Racing, focuses on evaluating the effectiveness of two distinct trajectory planning algorithms in the context of high-speed autonomous navigation. The study aims to analyze the advantages and limitations of each approach, comparing their computational efficiency, adaptability to dynamic environments, and overall performance in generating feasible and optimized racing lines. By conducting simulations and performance benchmarks, the research provides a data-driven assessment of how Delaunay and RRT* handle different track conditions, cone distributions, and real-time decision-making constraints. The goal is to determine which method offers superior reliability and efficiency for autonomous racing applications, while also exploring potential optimizations or hybrid strategies that could enhance path-planning robustness in competitive scenarios.
